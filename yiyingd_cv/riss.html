About RISS

The Robotics Institute at CMU, since its founding in 1979, has long been an international leader in robotics research and education. Despite being in the School of Computer Science, the department is “an intellectually diverse, multi-disciplinary department.” The Institute offers diverse opportunities up to intensive masters and PhD programs, working to cultivate the next generation of roboticists. It is the world’s largest university affiliated robotics research group, with over 100 projects and many partnerships with government, industry, and non-profit organizations.
The CMU Robotics Institute has a very special and selective program called the RISS - Robotics Institute Summer Scholars. RISS is an eleven week summer undergrad research program that accepts both U.S. and international applicants with financial aid. The program aims to provide students with an immersive experience in cutting-edge fields and and the opportunity to conduct research that has real world impact. For this program, they have about 30 labs this year that are accepting applicants for the program, and the Institute received over 750 applications this year.
